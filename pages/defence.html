NDA ( National Defence Academy)

1. The national defence academy is an iconic institution in the sphere of military institution.
2. It is one of the most prestigious armed forces.
3. In NDA cadets of three service, The Army, The Navy and the Air Force are trained together
4. It is the first tri service academy in the world.
5. The students who wish to join NDA they have to go through written exam followed by UPSC and followed by extensive interviews.
6. In the interview normal questions cover:
7. General aptitude
8. Psychological test
9. Team skills
10. Social skills
11. Age limit : 16 to 19 years

Indian Air Force

ABOUT AIR FORCE GROUP X & GROUP Y EXAM

Indian Air Force conducts Recruitment Exam for the Selection of Airmen in various trades of Technical as well as Non Technical Branches. As a soldier this is one of the best career choices for the youngsters who are willing to make a career in Indian Armed Forces.

INDIAN AIR FORCE

Out of the Three Battlefields i.e. Ground, Air & Water, the aerial defence system has assumed huge significance in the wake of technological advancements done in the last 2 decades. With advent of ICBM , Super Sonic Fighter Jets & Nuclear deployments. With Pakistan & China being tenacious neighbours, The Indian Air Force has a daunting task in hand to keep a check and maintain balance. In general whenever we are thinking of Air Force we assume that its all about Fighter Planes.

Group X & Group Y Trades:

Indian Air Force Recruits Technical & non Technical Staff through Group X & Group Y recruitment Process. The candidates who have done their 10+2 with Maths & Physics or have done diploma in engineering are eligible for Technical Group X trade. Those who have done 10+2 in any stream are eligible for Non Technical or Group Y trade.
